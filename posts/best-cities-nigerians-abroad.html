Best Cities for Nigerians in UK, Canada & Germany

JapaWise Team January 2025 14 min read

Ad loading...

Choosing where to settle is almost as important as choosing which country to relocate to. Different cities offer different job markets, costs of living, and Nigerian community sizes. This guide compares the best cities for Nigerian immigrants in the UK, Canada, and Germany based on job opportunities, affordability, and community.

United Kingdom

London

Pros:

  • Largest Nigerian community in UK
  • Most job opportunities across sectors
  • Higher salaries (with London weighting)
  • Excellent transport network
  • Cultural diversity
  • Nigerian shops, churches, and restaurants abundant

Cons:

  • Very expensive (rent, transport, daily living)
  • Competitive job market
  • Fast-paced, can be overwhelming

Best for: Those with higher-paying jobs, finance, tech, healthcare in major hospitals

Manchester

Pros:

  • Growing Nigerian community
  • Significantly cheaper than London
  • Strong job market (BBC, tech, healthcare)
  • Vibrant city with good nightlife
  • Good universities

Cons:

  • Smaller community than London
  • Weather (lots of rain)
  • Lower salaries than London

Best for: Those seeking balance between opportunity and affordability

Birmingham

Pros:

  • Large Nigerian community
  • Central UK location
  • Affordable housing
  • Healthcare and manufacturing jobs
  • Good transport links to London

Cons:

  • Fewer high-paying tech jobs
  • City center still developing

Best for: Healthcare workers, families, those wanting Nigerian community

Canada

Toronto

Pros:

  • Largest Nigerian community in Canada
  • Most job opportunities
  • Very multicultural
  • Financial hub
  • Nigerian restaurants, shops, churches

Cons:

  • Very expensive (especially rent)
  • Harsh winters
  • Competitive job market

Best for: Finance, tech, healthcare, those wanting strong Nigerian community

Calgary

Pros:

  • Growing Nigerian community
  • Lower cost of living than Toronto
  • No provincial sales tax
  • Oil and gas, tech jobs
  • Close to mountains

Cons:

  • Smaller community
  • Very cold winters
  • Less diverse job market

Best for: Engineers, tech workers, families wanting affordability

Edmonton

Pros:

  • Very affordable housing
  • Good healthcare job market
  • Growing Nigerian community
  • Strong economy

Cons:

  • Extremely cold winters
  • Smaller city feel
  • Less diverse job opportunities

Best for: Healthcare workers, those prioritizing affordability

Winnipeg

Pros:

  • Most affordable major city in Canada
  • Easier PR pathway through Manitoba PNP
  • Strong community support for immigrants

Cons:

  • Coldest major city in Canada
  • Smaller job market
  • Limited Nigerian community

Best for: Those prioritizing PR pathway and affordability

Germany

Berlin

Pros:

  • Most international German city
  • English widely spoken in tech sector
  • Vibrant startup scene
  • Relatively affordable for a capital
  • Diverse expat community

Cons:

  • Smaller Nigerian community
  • German language still important for daily life
  • Housing market very competitive

Best for: Tech workers, creative professionals

Frankfurt

Pros:

  • Financial hub of Germany
  • High salaries
  • International environment
  • Good transport (major airport)

Cons:

  • Expensive
  • Smaller city, less cultural life
  • German language important

Best for: Finance professionals, bankers

Munich

Pros:

  • Highest quality of life in Germany
  • Strong job market (BMW, Siemens)
  • Beautiful surroundings
  • Safe and clean

Cons:

  • Most expensive German city
  • More conservative
  • Smaller expat community

Best for: Engineers, automotive professionals

Comparison Summary

Factor Best City
Largest Nigerian community London, Toronto
Best affordability Winnipeg, Berlin, Manchester
Tech jobs London, Toronto, Berlin
Healthcare jobs All UK cities, Toronto, Edmonton
Finance jobs London, Toronto, Frankfurt

Conclusion

The best city for you depends on your profession, budget, and priorities. If Nigerian community is important, London and Toronto stand out. If affordability matters most, consider cities like Birmingham, Calgary, or Berlin. Research the job market in your specific field and connect with Nigerians already living in cities you're considering.

Remember: you can always move within a country once established. Many Nigerians start in one city and relocate as their circumstances change.
