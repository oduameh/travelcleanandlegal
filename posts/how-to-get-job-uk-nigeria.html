How to Get a Job in UK from Nigeria: Complete Guide

JapaWise Team January 2025 14 min read

Getting a UK job with visa sponsorship from Nigeria is challenging but absolutely achievable with the right strategy. Thousands of Nigerians successfully secure sponsored roles in the UK each year across healthcare, technology, engineering, finance, and other sectors. This guide reveals proven strategies for finding UK employment from Nigeria.

The key to success is understanding what UK employers are looking for, targeting companies with sponsor licenses, and presenting yourself effectively despite being overseas. Let's break down the entire process.

Understanding UK Visa Sponsorship

To work in the UK from Nigeria, you typically need an employer willing to sponsor your Skilled Worker Visa. Not all UK companies can sponsor‚Äîthey need a valid sponsor license from the Home Office.

What Employers Consider

  • Can this role be filled by a UK resident?
  • Does the candidate have exceptional skills?
  • Does the salary meet the threshold (¬£38,700 general, lower for some roles)?
  • Is the role genuinely skilled (RQF 3+)?
  • Is the cost and admin of sponsorship worthwhile?

Where to Find UK Jobs with Visa Sponsorship

Job Boards

  • Indeed UK: Search "[job title] visa sponsorship" or "[job title] skilled worker visa"
  • LinkedIn: Use filters and search for sponsorship mentions
  • Totaljobs: Major UK job board
  • Reed.co.uk: Wide range of industries
  • CV-Library: Another major UK job site
  • NHS Jobs: For healthcare roles

Company Career Pages

Apply directly on company websites. Major employers who sponsor include:

  • NHS Trusts (healthcare)
  • Big 4 accounting firms
  • Tech companies (Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft UK)
  • Financial institutions
  • Engineering firms
  • Multinational corporations

Official Sponsor List

The Home Office publishes a list of licensed sponsors. Download and research companies in your field that are licensed to sponsor.

Sectors Most Likely to Sponsor Nigerians

  • Healthcare: Nurses, doctors, pharmacists, radiographers, physiotherapists
  • Technology: Software developers, data engineers, DevOps, cybersecurity
  • Finance: Accountants, financial analysts, actuaries
  • Engineering: Civil, mechanical, electrical, petroleum
  • Education: Teachers (STEM subjects especially)
  • Care sector: Senior care workers

Creating a UK-Style CV

UK CVs differ from Nigerian CVs. Key differences:

  • Length: 2 pages maximum
  • No photo: UK CVs don't include photos
  • No personal details: No date of birth, marital status, or religion
  • Achievement-focused: Quantify your accomplishments
  • Tailored: Customize for each application
  • Professional email: Use firstname.lastname@gmail.com format

CV Structure

  1. Personal statement (3-4 sentences summarizing your value)
  2. Key skills
  3. Work experience (reverse chronological)
  4. Education
  5. Professional certifications

Writing Effective Cover Letters

Always include a cover letter for UK applications:

  • Address why you're suitable for this specific role
  • Demonstrate knowledge of the company
  • Briefly explain your visa situation (sponsored visa required)
  • Show enthusiasm and cultural fit
  • Keep it to one page

Interview Preparation

Technical Setup

  • Reliable internet (have backup ready)
  • Professional background
  • Good lighting and audio
  • Test technology beforehand

Content Preparation

  • Research the company thoroughly
  • Prepare STAR answers for competency questions
  • Have specific examples of achievements ready
  • Prepare questions to ask the interviewer
  • Practice speaking clearly and confidently

Common UK Interview Questions

  • "Tell me about yourself"
  • "Why do you want to work for us?"
  • "Describe a challenging situation and how you handled it"
  • "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
  • "What are your salary expectations?"

Addressing the Visa Question

Be upfront about needing visa sponsorship:

  • Mention it in cover letters
  • If asked, explain you require Skilled Worker Visa sponsorship
  • Emphasize your willingness to start as soon as visa is processed
  • Note that visa processing is typically 3 weeks
  • Highlight that you'll be a long-term employee (visa tied to employer)

Networking Strategies

  • Connect with Nigerians working in UK companies on LinkedIn
  • Join industry-specific groups
  • Attend virtual networking events and webinars
  • Request informational interviews
  • Ask for referrals (many companies have referral bonuses)

Timeline Expectations

Be patient‚Äîthe process takes time:

  • Job search: 3-12 months typically
  • Interview process: 2-6 weeks per company
  • Offer to CoS: 2-4 weeks
  • Visa processing: 3 weeks
  • Total: Plan for 6-18 months from start to arrival

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Applying to jobs that don't offer sponsorship
  • Using a Nigerian CV format
  • Generic applications (not tailoring CV/cover letter)
  • Poor interview preparation
  • Underselling your achievements
  • Being defensive about visa requirements
  • Giving up too early

Conclusion

Getting a UK job from Nigeria requires persistence, the right strategy, and professional presentation. Focus on industries actively recruiting internationally, target companies with sponsor licenses, and present yourself as a candidate worth the investment of sponsorship.

Many Nigerians have made this journey successfully. With determination and the right approach, you can join them.
