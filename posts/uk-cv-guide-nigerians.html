How to Write a UK-Style CV: Guide for Nigerian Job Seekers

JapaWise Team January 2025 10 min read

Your CV is your first impression with UK employers. Nigerian CVs and UK CVs have significant differences in format, content, and style. Converting your Nigerian CV to UK standards can dramatically improve your chances of getting interviews. This guide shows you exactly how.

Key Differences: Nigerian vs UK CV

Feature Nigerian CV UK CV
Photo Common Never
Date of birth Included Never
Marital status Included Never
Length 3-5 pages 2 pages max
Objective statement Common Personal statement instead
References Full details "Available on request"

UK CV Structure

1. Contact Information

  • Full name (no titles like Mr/Mrs)
  • Phone number (UK format preferred)
  • Professional email (firstname.lastname@gmail.com)
  • Location (city only, no full address)
  • LinkedIn profile URL

DO NOT include: Photo, date of birth, marital status, gender, nationality, religion

2. Personal Statement (3-4 sentences)

A brief summary of who you are professionally:

  • Your professional identity
  • Key skills and experience
  • What you're looking for
  • Tailored to each role

3. Key Skills

6-8 bullet points highlighting relevant skills:

  • Match skills to job description
  • Include both technical and soft skills
  • Be specific (not just "good communication")

4. Work Experience (Reverse Chronological)

Format for each role:

  • Job Title | Company Name | Location
  • Dates (Month Year - Month Year or Present)
  • 4-6 bullet points of achievements
  • Focus on impact, not just duties
  • Quantify where possible (numbers, percentages)

5. Education

  • Degree, Institution, Year
  • Relevant modules or dissertation (if recent graduate)
  • No need to list all O-Levels/A-Levels

6. Additional Sections (Optional)

  • Professional certifications
  • Languages
  • Volunteer work
  • Professional memberships

Achievement-Based Writing

Instead of: "Responsible for managing team"

Write: "Led team of 8 developers, delivering 12 projects on time and 15% under budget"

Use this formula: Action verb + What you did + Result/Impact

Strong action verbs:

  • Achieved, delivered, implemented, increased, reduced
  • Led, managed, coordinated, developed, established
  • Streamlined, optimized, transformed, generated, secured

Common Nigerian CV Mistakes

  • Too long: Keep it to 2 pages maximum
  • Including personal details: Remove photo, age, marital status
  • Duties not achievements: Focus on what you accomplished
  • Listing every job: Focus on relevant recent roles
  • Generic statements: Be specific and quantify
  • Poor formatting: Use clean, professional layout
  • Spelling errors: Use UK English spellings

UK English Spellings

Use British spellings:

  • organisation (not organization)
  • colour (not color)
  • centre (not center)
  • analyse (not analyze)
  • programme (not program)

Tailoring Your CV

For each application:

  1. Read the job description carefully
  2. Identify key requirements
  3. Match your experience to those requirements
  4. Use similar language/keywords
  5. Adjust your personal statement
  6. Reorder bullet points to highlight relevant experience

Conclusion

Converting your Nigerian CV to UK format is essential for job success. Remove personal details, keep it concise, focus on achievements, and tailor for each role. A well-formatted UK CV significantly increases your chances of getting interviews.
